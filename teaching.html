Teaching

In addition to the courses I taught as a lecturer at Boston University, I am regularly invited to speak in other courses at other schools, recently at Tufts and MIT, where I've discussed some of the projects I've listed here, held the train the trainer sessions for CAD instructors, and described how AR and IoT can be brought into the classroom, both as a subject and teaching tool.

Courses Taught at Boston University

Course Description / Responsibilities
Mechanical Engineering Capstone Experience
  • Coached students through research and design process for senior capstone projects.
  • Led workshops on ethics, entrepreneurship, project management, technical writing and presentation, and other professional engineering topics.
Electro-Mechanical Systems Design
  • Guided student teams through machine design as applied to a semester-long design problem.
Automation & Manufacturing Methods
  • Introduced automated manufacturing, production dynamics, SPC, real-time control theory, LEAN, CNC machining, collaborative robotics and DFM.
  • Led continuous improvement of hardware, software, and lab manuals supporting the course's three-hour labs.
Computer Aided Design & Machine Components
  • Taught CAD fundamentals: modeling, kinematic simulation, FEA, and production of properly tolerance drawings.
  • Introduced machine components, gear/bearing analysis, and coordinate/geometric dimensioning & tolerancing (GD&T) via active learning.
Freshman Seminar
  • Met regularly with small groups to broaden perspectives on college and foster understanding of engineering and ethical responsibilities.
Modern Physics
  • Introduced wave-particle duality, Schrödinger equation, quantum applications (diodes, LEDs, lasers) and principles of special relativity.
Product Design
  • Exposed students to iterative engineering design emphasizing DFM, human-centered design, sustainability and design communication.
Material Science
  • Explored material properties and processing, materials selection for design, and supervised student experiments on processing effects.
Mechanics of Materials
  • Taught structural mechanics principles and their relation to material properties.
Clean Energy
  • Created a freshman engineering course with hands-on exercises illustrating wind generation, solar heating, energy conversion and fuel cells.

Philosophy

What I cannot make, I do not understand.” - Richard Feynman
I often make things using techniques, tools, principles and technologies that are the subject of my teaching. In my experience, no description of how something works is complete, and it is only through making or repairing a thing that a person can fully understand the detail. Where possible, I incorporate this practice into my teaching, and often encourage students to do the same.
