Prototyping

I create prototypes for almost everything I do. From woodwork to 3-D printed scale models to machined functional devices, iterating with a physical prototype is an essential part of the product development process.

Prototyping is a powerful tool for learning and communication. It allows you to quickly test and validate ideas, and to share them with others in a tangible way. I use prototyping to explore design concepts, to troubleshoot problems, and to communicate my ideas to clients and collaborators.

Below are just a few examples of some of the prototypes I've designed and built.

Medical Device Prototyping

I have worked with several different doctors over the years on different medical device prototypes, from specialized catheters to devices that would be worn by a patient to provide emergency injections if they are incapacitated. I can't share details due to NDA limitations, but I continue to do this development work as a consultant today.

One small project I can share is shown below, where I created a very simple and cheap arduino-based device to measure heart rate and oxygen partial pressure. The purpose of this prototype was to test the accuracy of this particular sensor -- results were compared to commericial devices.

Educational Demonstration Prototyping

As part of my work at PTC, I was tasked with building an example of how an engineer might use the Internet of things to solve a real-world engineering problem, for a class of Harvard Business students. I designed and built a LEGO-based rotary airplane engine model, and used a Raspberry Pi with a motor hat to read data from a small accelerometer module that we mounted to the engine.

We used the Raspberry Pi to run a real-time fast fourier transform (FFT) analysis of the vibrations from the accelerometer to illustrate to the students that we could monitor and debug the vibration problems in a physical system using sensors and IoT. The students could add or remove small amounts of mass from the propeller using very small LEGO pieces and see the resulting change in the vibration frequency on the LCD screen as well as on an IOT dashboard we created for the demonstration.

Skateboard Propulsion Prototyping

Another project I've worked on over the years was creating a prototype for a "motorstick" -- battery powered stick/oar that would be used to help propel a skateboarder to prepare them for jumps tricks or just cruising along. The idea never took off, but I had a good time making a functional prototype for the customer.
