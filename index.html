Mechanical Engineering Innovator & Lecturer

The Boston University Automated Design & Manufacturing Lab (ADML):

Academic & Research Background: My science and engineering journey began with a degree in Physics to support NSF-funded solar observation and image analysis using IDL. I returned to academia for a PhD in Materials Science and Engineering, focused on the development of solid oxide fuel cells, and the design and engineering of high temperature reactors for electrochemical extraction of metals from molten salts. I translated this insight into a postdoctoral fellowship focused on development of a for monitoring nuclear waste recycling at Idaho National Lab.

Professional Background: 25 years of engineering experience including high-temperature Solid Oxide Fuel Cell research and molten salt reactor design, engineering curriculum design and teaching, automated manufacturing systems design, digital twin development with augmented reality and the internet of things, medical device prototyping, historic post-and-beam barn construction and rehabilitation, and construction project 3D visualization.

Technical Expertise: Hands-on skills in mechanical and manufacturing systems, PLC and industrial robot programming, high-temperature electrochemistry, coding (JavaScript, Python, IDL, Arduino), Computer-Aided Design (CAD), and fine woodworking.

Teaching and Mentoring: Lecturer and academic advisor with experience in designing and delivering engineering education programs, in-person and online. I'm honored to still hear from former students to this day, who reach out for career advice.

Currently advising the MIT Leap Group on CAD and Design for Manufacturing, for their TechAMP program to up-skill factory technicians. I helped to create and produce the Spoke #6: Design and Manufacturing Fundamentals curriculum to help learners achieve foundational skills in design and manufacturing using CAD tools, focusing on 3D modeling, 2D drawings, mechanical assemblies, and DFM principles. I was also an advisor for the development of the Digital Manufacturing Spoke, design to provide knowledge and skills for digital manufacturing technologies, including simulation, digital twins, and advanced data analytics.
