Advising

MIT LEAP Group

Currently advising the MIT Leap Group on CAD and Design for Manufacturing, for their TechAMP program to up-skill factory technicians.

I helped to create and produce the Spoke #6: Design and Manufacturing Fundamentals curriculum to help learners achieve foundational skills in design and manufacturing using CAD tools, focusing on 3D modeling, 2D drawings, mechanical assemblies, and DFM principles.

I am also an advisor for the Digital Manufacturing Spoke, designed to provide knowledge and skills for digital manufacturing technologies, including simulation, digital twins, and advanced data analytics.

the digital engineering spoke uses Node-RED software to teach the principles of Industrial IOT, I've used the same software to monitor my sailboat power systems. More detail on Github.

Philosophy

What I cannot make, I do not understand.‚Äù - Richard Feynman
I often make things using techniques, tools, principles and technologies that are the subject of my teaching. In my experience, no description of how something works is complete, and it is only through making or repairing a thing that a person can fully understand the detail. Where possible, I incorporate this practice into my teaching, and often encourage students to do the same.
