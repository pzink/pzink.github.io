Automated Design & Manufacturing Facility

In 2015 Boston University asked me to build a new Automated Design & Manufacturing Lab to support several courses at the university. Assessing the commercially available options, I decided to build, rather than buy. This allowed us to integrate more industrial scale machinery, and provided a wealth of opportunities for students to dig deeper into how the various components worked individually and as an integrated system. Inverted Pendulum

Made from a repurposed linear slide, encoders track the position of cart and angle of the pivot, and send feedback to an Arduino Mega which enables control of the cart position and pendulum angle. Arduino code based on this project.

In this case, there are two PID control loops: The outer loop aims to keep the cart position on the slide in the center; the controller output is the target pivot angle for the inner loop If the cart moves right, the target angle tilts left to cause the cart to move back to center The inner loop just aims to keep the pendulum vertical by moving the car to get the actual pivot angle to match the target angle.
